PUMP MOTOR

The Pump Motor is an actuator which is used to pump water or other fluids by converting electrical energy into mechanical energy. • All pumps use basic forces of nature to move a liquid. As the moving pump parts such as impeller, vane, piston, and diaphragm begin to move the air is pushed out of the way. The movement of air creates a partial vacuum (low pressure) which can be filled up by more air, or in the case of water pumps, water. • In theory, a pump increases the pressure of a fluid to give it the driving force required for flow.
